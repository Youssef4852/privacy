We collect personal information from you when you use our services, including your name, email address, IP address, and usage data. This helps us improve our services and offer a better experience. We collect this data automatically through cookies and similar tracking technologies. In some cases, you may voluntarily provide us with additional information, such as feedback or responses to surveys. This data allows us to improve our customer service and enhance user satisfaction.

Data Usage

The information we collect is used to personalize your experience, process transactions, and improve customer service. We do not share your information with third parties except where necessary to provide services. This information allows us to enhance your interactions with our platform by ensuring faster processing of your requests, providing relevant content, and improving overall service quality. Cookies

Our website uses cookies to enhance your experience. Cookies are small files stored on your device that help us understand how you use our site. These cookies allow us to remember your preferences, such as language or theme settings, and provide you with a more personalized experience. By collecting usage data, cookies help us improve the performance of our site, including faster load times and more relevant content. You can disable cookies in your browser settings. However, please note that disabling cookies may affect your user experience and limit certain functionalities of our website. For instance, some features may not work as intended, or you may need to log in repeatedly. We use both first-party cookies, which are set by our site, and third-party cookies, which are set by external services such as analytics or advertising platforms. These third-party cookies may track your browsing activity across different websites to offer personalized ads or services.

User Rights

As a user of InVision, you have certain rights that are protected under data protection laws. These rights give you more control over how your personal information is handled and ensure that you can manage your data in a way that aligns with your preferences. Understanding these rights is important for maintaining your privacy and security while using our services. Below, we outline your specific rights and how you can exercise them. Access to Personal Information: You have the right to request access to the personal data that we hold about you. This includes information such as your name, email address, IP address, and any other data we may collect. If you want to know what information we have stored about you, you can submit a request, and we will provide you with a copy of this data in a structured, commonly used, and machine-readable format. Right to Modify or Correct Information: If you notice that any of the personal data we have about you is inaccurate or incomplete, you have the right to request corrections or updates to this information. This ensures that your data remains accurate and up to date, which is critical for providing you with personalized and efficient services. Right to Deletion (Right to be Forgotten): You have the right to request the deletion of your personal information at any time. If you no longer wish to use our services or want to ensure that your data is completely removed from our systems, you can request that we erase your personal data. This right is not absolute, and there may be situations where we need to retain certain data for legal or business reasons, but in most cases, we will honor your request. Right to Restrict Data Processing: In some cases, you may request that we restrict the processing of your personal data. This means that, while we can still store your data, we will not actively use or process it for any specific purposes. This right can be exercised when you believe the data is inaccurate, the processing is unlawful, or you have objected to the processing of your data. Right to Object to Data Processing: You also have the right to object to the processing of your personal data. If we are processing your data based on legitimate interests, you can challenge that processing. Additionally, if we are using your data for direct marketing purposes, you can opt out of such communications at any time. Right to Data Portability: You have the right to receive your personal information in a structured, commonly used, and machine-readable format, and to transmit this data to another service provider without hindrance. This right applies only to data that you have provided to us and that is processed based on your consent or a contract. Right to Withdraw Consent: If we rely on your consent for processing your personal data, you have the right to withdraw that consent at any time. This will not affect the lawfulness of any processing that occurred prior to your withdrawal of consent. You can withdraw consent through our provided mechanisms, and we will ensure that your data is no longer processed for the purposes for which consent was previously given. Exercising Your Rights: To exercise any of these rights, please contact our support team at the contact information provided in the "Contact Us" section of this privacy policy. We will respond to your request within a reasonable timeframe and in accordance with applicable law.

Data Retention

At InVision, we take the storage and retention of your personal data seriously. We aim to retain your personal information for as long as necessary to fulfill the purposes for which it was collected, in accordance with this privacy policy. Below, we outline our approach to data retention and the factors that influence how long we keep your information. Retention Period Based on Purpose: We will retain your personal information only for as long as necessary to fulfill the purposes outlined in this privacy policy. This includes using your data for providing services, improving your experience, processing transactions, and complying with legal requirements. Once the data is no longer needed for these purposes, we will securely delete or anonymize it, depending on the type of data and our business needs. Legal and Regulatory Requirements: There may be situations where we are required by law to retain certain data for a longer period. For example, if applicable tax laws or financial regulations require us to retain financial records for a certain number of years, we will comply with those obligations. Similarly, we may need to retain data for legal disputes or to comply with any government investigations or regulatory audits. Account Deletion and Data Removal: If you choose to delete your account or request that we remove your personal data, we will do so in a timely manner. However, as mentioned earlier, we may retain some information in accordance with our legal obligations or for legitimate business purposes. This includes cases where we need to retain data to prevent fraud, enforce agreements, or resolve disputes. Data Anonymization: In some cases, we may anonymize your personal data. This means that we remove or alter information that can identify you, so that it can no longer be used to track or identify you as an individual. Anonymized data may still be retained for statistical, analytical, or research purposes, as it is no longer personally identifiable. Reviewing Data Retention Practices: We regularly review our data retention practices to ensure that we are complying with legal requirements and maintaining a high standard of data protection. If we find that any data is no longer necessary for our operations or that we have exceeded the retention period, we will take appropriate action to delete or anonymize that data. Data Disposal: When we no longer need to retain your personal data, we take steps to ensure its secure disposal. This may involve securely deleting electronic data, overwriting storage media, or securely shredding paper records. We strive to prevent unauthorized access to your data during the disposal process.
